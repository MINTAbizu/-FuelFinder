1. Executive Summary

FuelFinder is a digital coordination platform that helps users discover nearby fuel stations and EV charging stations, review station conditions, reserve queue access, activate service through payment, receive real-time alerts, and complete secure station check-in. It also gives station operators a dashboard for queue control, stock visibility, pricing updates, and customer-flow management.

The business is built around a clear operational pain point: users and stations currently operate in a high-friction environment with poor information visibility, manual queue handling, wasted travel, and disconnected payment and access workflows. FuelFinder converts that disorder into a structured digital service model.

Core thesis: FuelFinder is not just a map or reservation app. It is an operational layer between drivers, stations, payment systems, and queue management.

2. Company Overview

Vision

To become the leading digital coordination platform for fuel and vehicle-energy access in Ethiopia and later across regional African markets.

Mission

To reduce wasted time, uncertainty, congestion, and operational inefficiency by digitizing how users and stations coordinate access to fuel and EV charging services.

Current Product Footprint

  • Customer mobile app
  • Backend API and realtime queue engine
  • Owner and admin dashboard
  • Support for local payment pathways and Ethiopia-specific location structures

3. Problem Definition

Fuel and charging access in many urban markets is inefficient because users lack trustworthy, timely, and actionable information before they travel. Station operations are also burdened by manual queue handling, repeated customer inquiries, and weak digital coordination.

  • Drivers travel without knowing where fuel is available
  • Queues are long, manual, and difficult to predict
  • Users waste fuel and time moving between stations
  • Station operators lack a connected demand-management tool
  • EV charging access introduces similar uncertainty around readiness and availability

4. Solution Overview

FuelFinder provides one connected workflow that covers the major stages of station access:

  • Nearby station discovery
  • Station detail visibility
  • Queue reservation
  • Payment-linked reservation activation
  • Real-time alerts and turn notifications
  • Secure arrival check-in
  • Station-side operations management

20. Conclusion

FuelFinder has the structure of a serious platform business: a real and repeated customer problem, a clear software-enabled operational solution, recurring revenue potential, B2B and B2C growth pathways, and a strong local starting point with regional expansion potential.

Its business model is strongest when centered on station subscriptions, supported by transaction fees, and extended through fleet and promotional services. With disciplined rollout and product refinement, FuelFinder can grow from a strong technical project into a scalable commercial platform.
