# FuelFinder Business Plan

## 1. Executive Summary

FuelFinder is a digital platform designed to improve access to fuel stations and electric charging stations by combining station discovery, queue reservation, payment-linked activation, real-time alerts, and station-side operations management in one connected system.

The project addresses a major operational problem in Ethiopia and similar emerging markets: drivers often waste time, fuel, and effort searching for available fuel or charging access without reliable real-time information. FuelFinder reduces this uncertainty by helping users discover nearby stations, evaluate conditions, reserve access, complete payment, and receive queue updates before and during travel.

FuelFinder is more than a consumer mobile app. It is a multi-sided platform that serves:

- drivers and vehicle owners,
- fuel station operators,
- electric charging operators,
- fleet managers,
- and eventually public and private mobility ecosystems.

The business opportunity is strong because the platform solves a real daily pain point, supports both B2C and B2B revenue paths, and can expand from fuel access coordination into a broader vehicle-energy services marketplace.

## 3. Problem Statement

Fuel distribution and access to energy stations often involve major inefficiencies:

- users travel to stations without knowing fuel availability,
- long physical queues waste time and fuel,
- station information is scattered and often unreliable,
- queue management is manual and difficult to control,
- digital payment is disconnected from service access,
- station owners lack a unified digital operations tool,
- EV users also face uncertainty about charger availability and readiness.

These problems create:

- customer frustration,
- unnecessary transportation costs,
- increased congestion,
- reduced trust in service access,
- and poor operational visibility for station operators.

FuelFinder exists to solve this coordination gap.

---

## 4. Solution

FuelFinder provides a connected platform with the following capabilities:

- mobile station discovery,
- map-based fuel and EV station browsing,
- queue reservation,
- payment-linked reservation activation,
- real-time queue alerts,
- secure check-in and verification,
- owner dashboard for station operations,
- location-aware station directory management,
- support for local payment systems such as Telebirr and Chapa.

The platform creates value on both sides:

### For users
- less wasted time,
- fewer unnecessary trips,
- more trusted station information,
- more predictable access to service.

### For stations
- better queue control,
- improved customer flow,
- digital payment traceability,
- stronger operational visibility,
- improved customer trust.

## 5. Product Description

FuelFinder currently consists of three major product layers:

### 5.1 Mobile App
The mobile app allows users to:

- register and log in,
- browse nearby stations,
- choose fuel or EV station type,
- view station details,
- reserve queue access,
- make payments,
- receive alerts,
- view transaction history.

### 5.2 Backend Coordination Engine
The backend manages:

- authentication,
- queue logic,
- reservation status,
- station records,
- payment orchestration,
- notifications,
- real-time updates,
- and administrative data.

### 5.3 Owner Dashboard
The owner dashboard allows station managers to:

- view station activity,
- manage queue progress,
- manage staff,
- update stock and pricing,
- review payments,
- manage operations more effectively.

## 9. Revenue Model

FuelFinder can use a hybrid revenue model.

### 9.1 Station Subscription Model
Station operators pay a monthly or yearly subscription for access to the owner dashboard and station management tools.

Possible pricing tiers:

- Basic plan for single stations
- Professional plan for stations with more operational features
- Enterprise plan for multi-station operators and chains

### 9.2 Transaction Fee Model
FuelFinder earns a small service fee from queue reservations or digital payment transactions processed through the platform.

### 9.3 Premium Placement / Promotion
Stations can pay for promoted visibility, priority listing, or promotional offers inside the app.

### 9.4 Fleet and B2B Contracts
FuelFinder can license dashboard tools, reporting, and station access coordination to:

- fleets,
- delivery companies,
- transport businesses,
- institutional partners.

### 9.5 Data and Analytics Services
Over time, aggregated operational analytics can be packaged for enterprise customers, provided privacy and compliance standards are respected.

## 16. Risk Analysis

### Risk 1. Slow station adoption
Some stations may resist process change.

**Mitigation**
- offer pilot onboarding,
- provide simple dashboard training,
- show operational benefits clearly.

### Risk 2. Data quality and live updates
Users need reliable information for trust.

**Mitigation**
- start with curated pilot stations,
- use operator-confirmed updates,
- build verification workflows.

### Risk 3. Payment integration complexity
Local payment flows may introduce technical or process friction.

**Mitigation**
- support multiple payment providers,
- keep a fallback workflow,
- test with small pilots before scale.

### Risk 4. User habit change
Users may continue relying on manual station search.

**Mitigation**
- focus on obvious convenience,
- show time-saving benefits,
- run awareness campaigns around pilots.

### Risk 5. Early-stage funding constraints
Growth may require technical, operational, and sales investment.

**Mitigation**
- use phased rollout,
- prioritize the most valuable features,
- seek grants, competitions, and pilot sponsors.
