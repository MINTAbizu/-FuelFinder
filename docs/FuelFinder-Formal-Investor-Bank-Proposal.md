## 2. Executive Summary

FuelFinder is a digital platform designed to solve one of the most visible operational problems in urban mobility and energy access: the lack of reliable, real-time coordination between drivers and fuel or EV charging stations.

The platform combines:

- station discovery,
- queue reservation,
- payment-linked activation,
- real-time alerts,
- secure check-in,
- and station-side operations management.

FuelFinder is built as a multi-part product with:

- a customer mobile application,
- a backend coordination engine,
- and an owner dashboard for station operations.

The initial target market is Ethiopia, where fuel access uncertainty, long queues, and fragmented station information create a strong need for a solution that saves time, reduces waste, and improves operational order. The platform is also designed to expand into EV charging coordination and fleet energy-access services.

This proposal seeks funding to support:

- pilot deployment,
- product refinement,
- station onboarding,
- customer acquisition,
- operational setup,
- and early revenue generation.

**Funding requested:** `ETB 8,500,000`

**Primary use of funds:**
- product refinement and infrastructure,
- station onboarding and pilot rollout,
- staffing,
- sales and marketing,
- working capital.

## 3. Business Overview

### Business Name
FuelFinder

### Legal / Operating Concept
Technology-enabled mobility and station operations platform

### Core Offering
FuelFinder helps users:

- find nearby fuel stations or EV charging stations,
- view operational conditions,
- reserve queue access,
- make digital payments,
- receive alerts,
- and verify arrival securely.

It helps station operators:

- manage queue flow,
- control station data,
- manage pricing and stock visibility,
- monitor customer activity,
- and digitize operational processes.

### Vision
To become the leading platform for coordinated access to fuel and vehicle-energy services in Ethiopia and, later, across regional African markets.

### Mission
To reduce wasted time, uncertainty, and congestion by digitizing how users and stations coordinate access to fuel and charging services.

## 4. Problem Statement

In many cities, especially in high-demand fuel environments, users often face the following problems:

- no reliable information about which station currently has fuel,
- long physical queues,
- wasted time and repeated trips,
- poor visibility into station readiness,
- manual queue management,
- low trust in fragmented updates,
- and disconnected payment and service workflows.

This creates direct economic and social costs:

- fuel wastage,
- time loss,
- congestion,
- stress for consumers,
- and operational inefficiency for station operators.

FuelFinder solves this by making station access visible, structured, and digitally coordinated.

## 7. Business Model

FuelFinder will use a hybrid business model.

### 7.1 Subscription Revenue
Fuel stations and charging operators pay a recurring monthly subscription to use the operations dashboard and management tools.

### 7.2 Transaction Revenue
FuelFinder earns a service fee on digital reservations and selected payment flows processed through the platform.

### 7.3 Promotion Revenue
Stations can pay for promoted visibility, offers, and in-app campaign placement.

### 7.4 B2B Fleet Revenue
FuelFinder can provide reporting and coordinated energy-access services to fleet operators on a contract basis.

### 7.5 Future Analytics Revenue
Aggregated station and service insights may later support analytics products for enterprise customers, subject to privacy and compliance standards.

## 18. Break-Even Estimate

### Simple Break-Even Logic
Using the Year 1 and Year 2 structure, the business is expected to move toward break-even when it reaches roughly:

- `60 to 70 active subscribed stations`, and
- consistent monthly transaction volume above `18,000 to 20,000`

### Estimated Break-Even Timing
- projected between late Year 1 and mid Year 2, depending on station onboarding speed and cost discipline.
